My Favorite Technology

My favorite technology would be the personal computer I made in the summer of 2025.

It took me a shocklingly low amount of time to build the computer, about 2 days in total and around
5 hours of work. I am very pleased with how the entire operation and the end product turned out,
although it was fairly expensive. My favorite part, and the centerpiece to the computer would be the
NZXT Kraken Elite V2 LCD display and cooling system. It's the circle-shaped display visible through
the largest window, and I can basically display anything I want through the NZXT display software.
It's currently displaying my GPU's temperature (the big number) in the photo as 88 degrees F.
The top smaller number is displaying the CPU's temperature, and the lower number is displaying
the temperature of the liquid cooling system.

One spec for my computer would be the Ryzen 7 7800X3D CPU, or the central processing unit.
CPUs are the main way a computer executes instructions. The 7 for Ryzen 7 means that it's on
performance tier 7, and it runs on 8 cores. The 7 in 7800X3D meaning its generation, and the 8
meaning its performance tier in the 7th generation. The 00X3D means its base model also comes
with higher clock speeds and can handle 3D-modeling more efficiently for better gaming performance.
This rests under my NZXT display.

Here's some more of my favorite specs to my computer. My computer has a GeForce RTX 5070, which contains
my GPU, or graphics processing unit. This essentially makes visual computations much faster and therefore
leads to better gaming performance. This is the big metal bog labeled "GEFORCE RTX" and "msi". Another
important piece would be my 2 TB Samsung MBME storage, which hides behind my GPU. My Random Access
Memory are the two flashing white stripes, which are DDR5 Kingston Fury Beasts, with each stick being
8 GB each. All of these pieces rest on top of my B850 Gaming Plus Wifi MSI Motherboard. This is noticeable
by the dragon visual that exists on the top corner of the computer when looking inside. Motherboards
connect all of these specs together so it all runs as one cohesive unit. If the motherboard isn't
functioning properly, pieces that connect to the motherboard might become inaccessible.
